15,000-mile Life

1. Beginning: A Star was Born

Born in the southernmost and beautiful Island (Jeju) in South Korea, my life has never been smooth and easy ever since I left the island. Coming to the United States was when my 15,000-mile life to the United States from South Korea began.

Now that I am in Binghamton, New York, I have had more challenges and become more mature throughout my life. Below is the story of how I became who I am now through three important chapters in my life.

2. Army: Like a Movie Star

Mandatory Military Service for two years in South Korea was the toughest time of all, however based on belief and trust towards the people that I worked with, I was honorably discharged from the Army.

I was like the main character of the movie, "Devil Wears Prada." I knew nothing about the Army just as the fashion-less Andy Sachs worked for the demanding elite magazine editor Miranda Priestly. One way that I coped was thinking just like how my commanding officer (Colonel) thought. I put all the details about him such as his music taste into my daily journal.With the effort he had slowly acknowledged and liked me.

3. Career: Changing the World of Tax with Technology

During the two years of service, I chose my career in Tax Technology based upon self-reflection. I have always been interested in Accounting and my experience at the fast-growing startup opened my eyes toward the world of technology, which led me to combine two different fields.

The study has also never been easy, but I am glad to say that the journey towards the world of Accounting and CS has been interesting and satisfying for my academic interests.
